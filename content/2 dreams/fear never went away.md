The clock wasn’t ticking; it never stopped. Eyes remained open, wide awake like statues in gardens that cast the fiercest look at unwelcome visitors. Another moment passed; sleep returned. Nothing was missed or taken. Lights flickered along a path, blocked from entering the mind.

It failed again, like every other night. Still alive on this planet.

Fingers moved involuntarily. A voice inside recalled the heavy mass far below the breathing torso. The bottle lay on its side, nearly emptied. Water kept dripping, knocking against ticking that slowly rose to awareness. Lips were dry from a hot night.

Another nightmare delivered incomprehensible fear. The nose brushed against sweat-coated hair as it tried to adjust its neck. Muscles around the spine began releasing, fluids returning to parts long numb. Fingertips felt dull as they gripped the duvet. Red stains traced their path from the night before. A palm wiped below the nose, feeling a thin crust melt into warm skin.

It stared at the ceiling.

The big toe screamed a reminder of neglected pain. New skin, too fragile, hadn’t survived rough thrashing through the night. Should’ve refilled the meds—but no pain seemed worse than the one already endured.

It gasps. Cold air mixed with the stench of sweat makes its way to the senses. It needs to get up.

Bloody fingers stain the bed further. The ground spins, tricking its mind before it can register the helplessness—its body unable to hold itself together, clinging only to the duvet before collapsing to the floor.

Now it hears the ticking. The small cubic clock has been under the bed the whole time. It must have knocked the poor thing over in its sleep. Photos are scattered everywhere.

He wasn’t smiling in any of them. He had just gotten out of bed, sinking into the couch, waiting for the sun to rise.

The mind is now fully awake with emotion. Reality does horrify.

“One day, all of this will end. But I cannot wait until then.”

As if the world is crumbling apart because it is still consiously alive.
