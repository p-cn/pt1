I walked past the monument of our past. Everything was gone except a shadow kept on luring behind the pages. I clacked on a leaf.

We was lying on the couch of summer, listening to the odd sound of that wind which breezes over and over out the window. I wasn’t scared. He tucked my arms in the duvet, his hand squeezed mine with such tenderness never before.

His face was unclear.

He walked through the corridor, found me lying on the floor with such blood all over the place. He carried me up. My brain was numb, my legs were paralyzed from the poison I took. He did not say a thing; his hand was between my forehead and his as he smiled at me as if nothing wrong happened.

He was watching me as I sat still on the couch. I waited for him to move so I can go back but only his breath was the thing that laid. I sighed. His hand caught mine in a moment when we were walking on the street and I was excitedly explaining the newest thing I wrote about him. He said it was okay to not write about him though he finds it amusing.

I stayed outside the garden with my plate of lunch. The swing never stopped moving. He came out of nowhere, held the chain and touched away my fear as I only knew he was back. He said he was sorry for keeping me waiting that long. It felt like months and years.

I recognized his features now as someone I know. The name was familiar and disturbing. He said he wanted to apologize for not telling when we first met. He said he knew that feeling dancing his heart and there were nights he couldn’t sleep thinking of me when he didn’t know I’d come a long way to be next to him like that.

His shoulders were not that broad and tall. His skin was not so rough that tainted the hug. His breath was so warm against my neck. He didn’t wish me to be happy as much as he should. He said it’s okay to feel hurt again and again as long as I stay with him and he had no intention of wiping that away. He knew I loved and that was no sin to crush oneself to the hopeless path of dreaming to escape. He only wanted me to walk out of that dream to him.

“You are so fit to me, so right…”
