the light that flickers,<br>through these glasses<br>shines on these bodies<br>

the music that plays in our old corners<br>upbeat and happy<br>people dancing and kissing<br>clinging for a desire to live<br>

the smoke that escapes up high<br>breath left with traces of blood<br>and here I stand watching<br>

the city is dead<br>cheap wine and fake people<br>flooded like an ocean of madness<br>the cool, the good-looking, and the lovely<br>
here I stand, being sad, indecent and lonely<br>

the man who lived in this city left long ago<br>the people we used to know<br>used to know<br>the girl used to walk by my side<br>she turned the corner long ago<br>

the roads where shades warmed out head and heated our kisses<br>the houses where I had your head leaning on mine<br>demolished and burns<br>

where in this city<br>are our hearts?<br>where in this city<br>do traces of our soul untouched?<br>each time I try coming back<br>I am reminded of the time that passed<br>

while I hold onto memories of you to live<br>this day speaks to me<br>without you, the city is no longer alive<br>

if only you knew<br>the way I can no longer feel<br>if only you could see me now<br>with my heart beating but my mind weeping<br>if only you could say my name<br>and take all this pain away<br>because no other words would do<br>and nobody else would be able to
