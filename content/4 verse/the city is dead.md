it’s the light that flickers,
reflected in the glass door
shines the life for these people
it’s the music that plays in our old corners
upbeat and happy
people dancing and kissing each other
clinging onto this life
it’s the smoke that escapes those windows up high
where breaths are left with traces of blood
and here I stand watching them
the city is dead with cheap wine and fake people
they all sank into the ocean of madness
they call the cool, the good-looking, and the lovely
no places for the sad, the indecent, the lonely
here I stand, being sad, indecent and lonely
the man that lived in this city left long ago
the people we used to know
used to know
the girl that I used to walk by her side
does not follow me anymore
the roads where shades warmed out head and heated our kisses
the houses where I had your head leaning on mine
demolished and burns
where in this city, are our hearts allowed to beat?
where in this city, do traces of our soul remains untouched?
each time I try coming back
I am reminded of the time that passed
while I cling onto memories of you to live
this day speaks to me
without you, the city is no longer alive
if only you knew the way I can no longer feel
if only you could see me now with my heart beating but my mind weeping
if only you could say my name and take all this pain away
because no other words would do
and no other person would be able to
