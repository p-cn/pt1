Do you know the feeling when you miss someone and that person seems to be right in front of you but you know that it’s not really them? You think that you have been in a dream where they were still around, only a few steps away; but the memory comes back and reminds you of the goner. Every time you pass an old place your steps become hasty and your heart seems to skip another beat. You are afraid to remember, to think of even just a piece of happiness you used to have.

You were living in a dream with them, the dream that now is coming back and haunting in your every single thought you have for them. You believed in pure love, in pure friendships without being hurt or bruised. But you just didn’t know that your life would be complete for having them.

You head is held high, looking arrogant at the past, telling the world that you will be fine without them. But every time their shadow comes in front of you, you go lost in the dream that you have dreamed so many times before. Your subconscious starts to spot any sign of them in the crowd, you know it’s hopeless but you’d do it anyway; because that one time when the shadow turned around, that one dead feeling and happiness begin to dance back inside; so you believe, one day their shadows become them and there isn’t a face that you’d waste only to see them again.

The ground under your feet breaks. Your senses go back to reality where the crowd is bursting into fierce lies, saying that they’re gone. You know it’s true but you can’t deny you’re expecting their coming back at the same place, the same time.

A childish vow you make, to be good and maybe at another time, another place for the same feeling of this odd expectation you’d die to feel it again.

“Goodbye my lover.
Goodbye my friend.
You have been the one.
You have been the one for me.
And I still hold your hand in mine.
In mine when I’m asleep.
And I will bare my soul in time,
When I’m kneeling at your feet.
Goodbye my lover.
Goodbye my friend.
You have been the one.
You have been the one for me.”
