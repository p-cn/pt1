There was an arrow; a thick arrow like ones in direction boards. It was pointing to the left but that wasn’t making any sense. I couldn’t turn my head to there because something was blocking my right side. I felt aching in my head. My eyes couldn’t look to anywhere else except that dark arrow behind those smokes. But there wasn’t only an arrow, but also small squares and rectangles; there I recognized them making sense.

It was a house. The smoke blocked most of the view for me to see whether somebody was in it. There was no light so I figured none. My vision crumbled on its own only to inform me that some time had passed again and here I was, not moving for one bit.

I smelt the soil, thick ugly smoke raging in my nose as I tried to blink. My eyes were dry and I could feel dirt clinging to my skin coming to get it. The sky and horizon were unclear drew me in another out-of-reality as my eyes stayed wide open. There was loud noises like explosions somewhere around here. There were shadows running across the field in front of my eyes from above to below like a funny gravity trick, except it wasn’t.

“We can’t be God nor Demon. We’re only human…”

Somebody was shaking my shoulder. I couldn’t see his face since dirt was covering most of the skin, eyes and mouth were only blurry shadow. He was saying something and repeating so many times. “Get me out of here…”

He left when my responses were empty, just like the scene now. The house was gone and noises of explosions became thinner. A cold flow was running from one side of my neck to the front and absorbed by my shirt, leaving the mark fiercely visible. For a moment, I was wondering if time had frozen. Nobody to answer me but weak responses of frigidity and dirt in my limbs had got my attention. My torso twitched for a second or so, gasped for air in this smoky space when my muscles now had become useless.

“We don’t breathe the same but we bleed the same…”

Something moved. Probably just a fallacy of my eyes.

Another explosion took place, cleared the smoke out in a moment. The shadow was moving towards me. Even in dreams I never fail to recognize that manner.

“Finally…”

Something clutched my heart as I let out the remaining air stuck in my chest for the whole time. I thought I had forgotten his face completely but I guess, I only buried it somewhere in my mind. Such dark eyes that once fooled me into befriending him. He looked around only for moment before crossing the fences, coming to me. I knew that evil smile when he found what he wanted, bewitching like a spell.

But he couldn’t be here. He didn’t exist anymore. Just that, I remembered those empty days after he left; only they didn’t scare me now but there was a relief to watch him approaching me not in the form of a ghost. He lied down just next to me. These thick brows had drawn themselves real, these blue eyes that contained the ocean and the sky in them. After all this time when I thought I could forget and he was gone for eternity; there he was like those empty days were never real.

“Here I am and don’t be scared.”

“Everything about you resonates happiness. Everything about you is how I want to be…”

His voice never stopped resounding, like a nightmare in daylight, a hallucination in real life. His shadow never stopped lurking around every corner I looked, only to remind me of the reality in which he was gone. He was happiness, he was what I want to be, but he was gone as well. My time came around and I thought I saw him every dead second but nothing ever feels real like this. I wanted to reach out. I wanted to make sure that he is not an illusion this time. My eyes were still dry and I couldn’t feel my hand at all.

“You don’t have to say anything.”

His hand touched my face, soothing my sore wounds in the gentlest way nobody ever could. His smile never went away as if something satisfied him and it must be long awaited for him and in this life for me. My lips grew cold. The surface of my skin started to crack as if my body was only layers of rotten clay wrapped in clothes, and the air is invading each little space inside.

Smoke in the air was completely gone, the house disappeared and explosions departed into nothingness. All I could see at this moment was his eyes looking back at me. I could feel his face with my fingers running across every part I treasured. After all those years, I never stopped knowing him and memories weren’t there to haunt me anymore. Instead, this non-reality had erased all the pain and cured all the open wounds given on me.

“We said life is a gift we never asked for; then it would mean I never asked for you and this moment would be so wasted. If in the end we all could be together then let this gift unfold.”

Gone the sun, gone the dark, gone the dream, and gone the fear. My mind was never more empty like this moment when I feel his skin on my fingers which weren’t numb anymore. His hand reached to my eyes and caressed them as I felt the door back to the world had closed, just like my real eyes.
