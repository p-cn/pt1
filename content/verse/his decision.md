He said he wanted to be free but he caged himself back in that prison. He said he had the ambition but he was too scared to make it. But what made his aura captivating and charming as a mature man was the certainty that ran in his way, simply said: “Just not my place.”

Like a blind bird, he flew from place to place to find the right one for him. At least he knows now which ones aren’t right though his soul never feels resting. Impatiently, he left the cage again. But this time he flew away too far to be found that he felt his freedom now was only another sin than being who he is.

What would he do to have his confidence back? Or the question should be if he really wanted it back for someone else’s sake. He knows they would follow his path no matter if it’s wrong or right. But as much as people may care, he made the decision, which would be the end without fear nor hope, without dreams or destination.

Which should be right, has been set right. Yet he never believed in God but he wants to be saved now. Fears have returned and shattered his dignity like thin glass. To escape, he takes the coward’s run.
